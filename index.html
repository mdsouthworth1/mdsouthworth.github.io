Recent News

Artificial Intelligence and machine learning continue to expand in popularity as their functionality and accuracy improve. Most of the advances in efficiency and productivity are likely to come from these technologies. Since the concerns about the rise of AI tend to be job displacement and obsolescence, companies need to implement AI so that it improves value but still allows for displaced workers to use current skills or learn new ones to adapt to the changing landscape. AI will create incredible value for consumers and companies and provide new opportunities for workers. These workers need to understand how to use the advancement of AI to improve productivity and lessen the impact of job displacement. This will likely lead to greater demand for social and emotional skills to focus on improving the jobs that are not automated. The shift will cause major disruption to those unable to adapt and most prone to automation. Low-skilled jobs, particularly in the South and industrial areas, are likely to see the most changes. AI will improve the jobs and productivity of workers that remain, and the result will be a more competitive job market. Therefore, it is imperative that these individuals learn the importance of socio-emotional needs to differentiate themselves from the workforce and create relationships to improve prospects. Developing these skills will be essential to survive and adapt to the upcoming changes. Those locations are also likely to experience large shifts in labor movement and loss of growth without reskilling. Since highly skilled and technical workers will experience an increase in demand, workers in repetitive industries such as automobile manufacturing will benefit from education focused on work less prone to automation. Current trends with high job growth include mainly technical or care-focused roles which will experience more demand and competition as automation advances. These workers should focus on health care or fields that require high levels of human decision-making and thinking. Analytics and data science roles can provide a haven for those experiencing a job loss from AI. Even skilled roles such as programming and data management jobs are increasingly being taken over by AI, showing that even previously highly skilled jobs have the potential to be given to AI. These trends show the importance of a need for a human element and judgment that AI cannot perform. Geographic locations such as the South that typically lack this focus will likely continue to experience declines in wealth and emigration as education is severely lacking in this area. Meanwhile, cities and countries that focus on education in science, technology, engineering, and health care may see the most growth. As the demand grows, so will the need for education. Automation will likely cause a seismic shift in world wealth and immigration patterns. AI and automation will improve society and create trillions in value but workers need to plan and adapt to maintain their standard of living.
