    Neural Network Classification

    Neural networks have become increasingly popular as hardware becomes advanced enough to utilize them and as advances in research have led to better models and libraries. AI is an extremely useful tool to analyze and classify data. This example seeks to determine how to classifly heartbeats using clinical data and classify them. The results of this analysis can be used by clinicians to quickly identify and treat individuals faster and provide better care and quality results while reducing potential mistakes by treatment providers.

    This data set is available by MIT. This is a multiclass classification model to determine if heartbeats are normal or contain abnormalities.The dataset contains measurements with 80 time steps for each row and each time step has one measurement. They are labeled from T1 to T80 and classified in the following categories:
    0 = Normal
    1 = Supraventricular premature beat
    2 = Premature ventricular contraction
    3 = Fusion of ventricular and normal beat
    4 = Unclassifiable beat

    In this example I use a variety of neural network models to find which produces the best accuracy. I first start with loading and exploring the dataset.


    Since the dataset does not need any transformation or cleaning I can immediately split into training. Next, I reshape the data for keras input and find the baseline accuracy.

    Now we can create our models. We initialize a sequential deep model and define our parameters ensuring that the final layer is equal to the number of classes that we have.

    I continue this with 2 LSTM models and a deep GRU model to get the following results:

    Each type of neural network is useful for different tasks. For this set Recurrent Neural Networks (RNN) and deep networks are more suited for sequential data, leading to its much greater accuracy. We could also change hyperparameters and use other models to see how they affect results. Grid search is a powerful tool for finding the best combination. For now, our accuracy is far higher than baseline and could still be improved.

    Price Projections with SAS

    In this project I use SAS to analyze and predict used car prices. Using various methods and data sources, I seek to project prices with publicly available data using regression. This shows how we can determine the influence various predictors have on a target variable, in this case price. We can then use this information to make better pricing decisions and anticipate the affect on demand.

    Using publicly available data, I use SAS to conduct linear regression on a dataset of cars sold in the US. The data includes parameters such as make, model, year, mileage, location, and features of over 30,000 vehicles.

    After exploring the data, I omit any observations with null values and check for skewness along with other abnormalities.

    Here we also see the differences in vehicle type.As expected, price is right skewed, and the most expensive vehicles tend to be SUVs and trucks, leading to most manufacturers focused on selling these types. Since the price is significantly skewed, log transformation remedies this issue. Based on the output, we also find insight that electric vehicles have higher selling pounts. With inflation high, the average new vehicles are selling at just over $47,000.

    AI and workforce displacement

    Artificial Intelligence and machine learning continue to expand in popularity as their functionality and accuracy improve. Most of the advances in efficiency and productivity are likely to come from these technologies. Since the concerns about the rise of AI tend to be job displacement and obsolescence, companies need to implement AI so that it improves value but still allows for displaced workers to use current skills or learn new ones to adapt to the changing landscape. AI will create incredible value for consumers and companies and provide new opportunities for workers. These workers need to understand how to use the advancement of AI to improve productivity and lessen the impact of job displacement. This will likely lead to greater demand for social and emotional skills to focus on improving the jobs that are not automated. The shift will cause major disruption to those unable to adapt and most prone to automation.

    Low-skilled jobs are likely to see the most changes. AI will improve the jobs and productivity of workers that remain, and the result will be a more competitive job market. Therefore, it is imperative that individuals learn the importance of socio-emotional needs to differentiate themselves from others in the workforce and create relationships to improve employment prospects. Developing these skills will be essential to survive and adapt to the industry transformation. Those locations are also likely to experience large shifts in labor movement and loss of growth without reskilling.

    Since highly skilled and technical workers will experience an increase in demand, workers in repetitive industries such as automobile manufacturing will benefit from education that is focused on work less prone to automation. Current trends with high job growth include mainly technical or care-focused roles which will experience more demand and competition as automation advances. These workers that focus on health care or fields require high levels of human decision-making and thinking. Analytics and data science roles can provide a haven for those experiencing job loss from AI.

    Even skilled roles such as programming and data management jobs are increasingly being taken over by AI, showing that even previously highly skilled jobs have the potential to be given to AI. These trends show the importance of a need for a human element and judgment that AI cannot perform. Geographic locations such as the South that typically lack this focus will likely continue to experience declines in wealth and emigration as education is severely lacking in this area. Meanwhile, cities and countries that focus on education in science, technology, engineering, and health care may see the most growth. As the demand grows, so will the need for education. Automation will likely cause a seismic shift in world wealth and immigration patterns. AI and automation will improve society and create trillions in value but workers need to plan and adapt to maintain their standard of living.

    Higginbotham, T. et al. (2020) HUMAN-CENTERED ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE AND WORKFORCE DISPLACEMENT. rep.
